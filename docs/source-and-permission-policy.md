# JainWorld Source and Permission Policy

## Core Rule

Credit is not the same as permission.

JainWorld may acknowledge a source, but that does not automatically mean the material can be hosted, embedded, copied, quoted at length, or republished.

## Allowed Content Types

- Original JainWorld summaries and metadata
- Public-domain material after review
- Creative Commons material that clearly permits the intended use
- Official source links
- Permission-received content with a clear record of the permission status
- Metadata-only references for books, audio, video, images, temples, calendars, and resources

## Not Allowed Content Types

- Copyrighted books or PDFs hosted without verified rights
- Copyrighted bhajan, aarti, stavan, or pravachan lyrics
- Random internet images
- Full articles copied from third-party sites
- Audio or video uploads without clear permission
- Temple, trust, government, or scholarship claims without verification

## Public Domain Rules

- Confirm that the work is actually public domain in the relevant context.
- Record `source_name`, `source_url`, `license_status`, and `attribution_text`.
- Keep `review_status` as `needs_review` until a human reviewer confirms the claim.

## Creative Commons Attribution Requirements

- Record the exact license name when known.
- Record the license URL when known.
- Keep creator/source attribution visible in the record.
- Note any edits under `changes_made` when images or media are adapted.

## Wikimedia Commons Reuse Checklist

- Verify the file page, creator, and license.
- Confirm that the license allows the intended use.
- Record the attribution text exactly enough for later display.
- Do not reuse the file if rights or attribution terms are unclear.

## Book / PDF Hosting Checklist

- Do not host full books by default.
- If rights are unknown, use:
  - `license_status = needs_review`
  - `permission_status = needs_review`
  - `hosting_allowed = false`
  - `external_link_only = true`
- Prefer metadata-only entries until the rights are documented.

## Audio / Bhajan / Aarti Permission Checklist

- Do not add copyrighted lyrics.
- Do not embed or host audio unless source and permission are clear.
- Record source, permission, and attribution before public use.
- If uncertain, keep the entry metadata-only.

## Image Attribution Format

Every external or reusable image record should aim to track:

- creator
- source_name
- source_url
- license_name
- license_url
- attribution_text
- changes_made
- permission_status
- review_status

## Temple and Resource Verification Rules

- Do not invent official contacts, schedules, addresses, deadlines, or benefits.
- Use link-only or metadata-only handling when source verification is incomplete.
- Keep review notes visible for future editorial checks.

## Status Definitions

### `review_status`

- `verified`: reviewed and considered safe for the current public use
- `approved`: reviewed for limited use
- `needs_review`: not fully verified yet
- `pending_review`: waiting for a reviewer
- `rejected`: not suitable for current use

### `permission_status`

- `allowed`: permission is clear for the intended use
- `needs_review`: permission is unclear or incomplete
- `not_allowed`: do not publish or host
- `metadata_only`: safe to keep only as a metadata/reference record

## Takedown and Correction Workflow

1. Record the concern in `corrections.html` or the review queue.
2. Mark the affected item `needs_review` or `not_allowed`.
3. Remove hosted or displayed content if necessary.
4. Preserve the review note for audit history.
5. Rebuild the search seed if public metadata changed.
## OnlineJainPathshala reminder

- User-reported mutual discussion is not the same as blanket publishing permission.
- Keep source credit visible.
- Use metadata, summaries, and review queues first.
- Do not host full books, PDFs, audio, or video without documented permission.
