# Panchang Archive Policy

## Purpose
- The Panchang archive exists as a source reference layer, not as blind public date truth.
- Month-wise scans help reviewers cross-check observances before structured dates are added.

## Core rules
- Do not OCR dense Panchang pages directly into final public dates.
- Do not remove visible branding, credit, or publisher references.
- Do not publish guessed or unsupported observance dates.
- Treat scan-based dates as `source_provided` until manually reviewed.

## Source-provided vs verified
- `source_provided`: a scan, page image, or source-backed page exists, but JainWorld has not completed manual extraction and review.
- `verified`: a date has source backing, review notes, scope, and last-verified details.

## Manual extraction workflow
1. Copy the month image or back-page PDF into the project archive.
2. Create or update an extraction queue record.
3. Review the scan manually.
4. Extract only the target observances needed.
5. Add source name, source note, tradition scope, and location scope.
6. Mark the structured date as review-first until editorial confirmation is complete.

## Local verification
- Users should still verify important observances with local sangh or trusted panchang before religious decisions, travel, or fasting plans.
